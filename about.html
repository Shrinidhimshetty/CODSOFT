ABOUT UDUPI

Udupi (Kannada: [uɖupi]) also known as 'Odipu' (Tulu: [oɖipu])[4] is a city in the Indian state of Karnataka. It is the administrative headquarters of Udupi district, and one of the fastest-growing cities in Karnataka. Udupi is one of the top tourist attractions in Karnataka and has various educational institutions. It is notable for the Krishna Temple and is also known as the temple city.[5] It also lends its name to the popular Udupi cuisine, is also known as Parashurama Kshetra,[6] and is famous for Kanakana kindi. [7] A centre of pilgrimage, Udupi is known as Rajatha Peetha pura

Udupi city is part of the eponymous district in Karnataka, India. As per the census in 2011, Udupi city has 33,987 households and a total population of 144,960, of which 71,614 are males and 73,346 are females.[10] The population of scheduled castes is 8,385 while that of scheduled tribes is 6,774.[10] The city population is growing at a rate of 14.03%.[10] Languages of Udupi (2011)[11] Tulu (52.1%) Kannada (16.95%) Konkani (15.01%) Urdu (6.04%) Marathi (3.42%) Malayalam (2.01%) Tamil (1.07%) Others (3.4%) The most spoken language in Udupi town is Tulu. Kannada and Konkani are also spoken in Udupi town. Dakhini Urdu and Beary are spoken by Muslims in the region.[citation needed]
